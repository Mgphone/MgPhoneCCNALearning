Day 22: RSTP Deep Dive

CCNA 200-301 Mastery Dashboard (802.1w)

🏎️

Timers vs Negotiation

Classic STP is painfully slow because it relies on passive timers. RSTP is fast because it relies on active negotiation.

Classic STP (The Staring Contest)

Switches sit in silence for 15 seconds (Listening) and another 15 seconds (Learning). They literally just wait for timers to expire before deciding a link is safe to use.

RSTP (The Handshake)

RSTP switches explicitly ask each other: "Can we form a link without making a loop?" If they agree, the port skips timers entirely and goes straight to Forwarding.

New BPDU Behavior

In 802.1D, only the Root Bridge generates BPDUs. In 802.1w (RSTP), EVERY switch generates its own BPDUs every 2 seconds. They act as active keepalives.

🤝

Proposal & Agreement (The Sync)

How do two RSTP switches instantly turn a link on without causing a loop? Through the Proposal/Agreement sync process.

1. A new link comes up. Both switches start in Discarding state.
2. SW1 (closer to the Root) sends a BPDU with the Proposal flag set.
3. SW2 receives it. To ensure no loops form, SW2 performs a Sync: it blocks ALL of its other non-edge ports.
4. Once SW2 is "safe" (synced), it sends an Agreement BPDU back to SW1.
5. Both sides immediately transition the link to Forwarding!
6. SW2 then repeats this process down the line with its downstream neighbors.
🔗

RSTP Link Types

For the Proposal/Agreement process to work, RSTP must categorize the physical connection. It relies on the duplex of the port.

  • Point-to-Point (P2p)

    Any port operating in Full-Duplex. RSTP assumes this is a direct switch-to-switch link. Proposal/Agreement is ONLY allowed on P2p links!

  • Shared (Shr)

    Any port operating in Half-Duplex. RSTP assumes this is connected to a legacy Hub. Proposal/Agreement is disabled. Must rely on slow classic timers.

  • Edge Port (P2p Edge)

    A port configured with PortFast. Transitions to forwarding immediately, skips Proposal/Agreement entirely.

⏱️ Fast Failure & Topology Changes

3x BPDU Miss = Dead

In classic STP, a switch waits 20 seconds (Max Age) to declare a neighbor dead. In RSTP, if a switch misses 3 consecutive BPDUs (6 seconds), it ages out the BPDU information and takes immediate action.

Topology Change (TC)

When a topology changes, switches must flush their MAC tables so traffic isn't sent to a dead link. In RSTP, the switch detecting the change floods TC BPDUs directly out all its forwarding ports, clearing MACs instantly.

🪝 Brain Hooks

Full Duplex = The Highway

RSTP knows that Full-Duplex (P2p) means exactly two devices are on the cable. It's safe to negotiate! Half-Duplex (Shr) means a hub is there with potentially many devices. It's too dangerous to fast-forward, so it falls back to slow timers.

Rapid PVST+

Cisco's default implementation is Rapid Per-VLAN Spanning Tree Plus. It runs a completely separate RSTP instance for every single VLAN.

⌨️ RSTP CLI & Verification

Switch# show spanning-tree Look at the top line: "Spanning tree enabled protocol rstp". Also look at the 'Type' column on the far right (it will display P2p, Shr, or P2p Edge).
Switch(config-if)# spanning-tree link-type point-to-point Manually forces the link type to P2p, overriding the duplex detection. (Rarely needed unless dealing with misconfigured duplex settings).
Switch(config-if)# spanning-tree portfast In `show spanning-tree`, this command changes the link type from 'P2p' to 'P2p Edge'.
Switch(config)# spanning-tree mode rapid-pvst Globally enables RSTP in the Cisco environment.

🔧 Troubleshooting

RSTP link is slow to come up

Symptom: Switch-to-switch link takes 30 seconds to come up despite running RSTP.

Fix: Check show spanning-tree. If the type says "Shr" (Shared), the port is operating in half-duplex. Proposal/Agreement is disabled on Shr links. Fix the duplex mismatch to full-duplex!

⚡ Quick Quiz (Active Recall)

Click questions to reveal answers.

1. What active negotiation mechanism does RSTP use to bypass the Listening and Learning timers on switch-to-switch links? ▼
The Proposal and Agreement process. Switches explicitly agree the link is loop-free before instantly moving to the Forwarding state.
2. If an RSTP port is operating in Full-Duplex, what link type does RSTP automatically assign to it? ▼
A Point-to-Point (P2p) link.
3. How long does it take for an RSTP switch to consider a neighbor dead after it stops receiving BPDUs? ▼
3 missed BPDUs, which equals 6 seconds (3 x 2-second hello timer).
4. In classic STP, only the Root Bridge generates BPDUs. Which switches generate BPDUs natively in RSTP? ▼
Every switch. All RSTP switches generate and send their own BPDUs every 2 seconds to act as a keepalive mechanism.
